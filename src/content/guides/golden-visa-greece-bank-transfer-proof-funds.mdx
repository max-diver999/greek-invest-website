Proof of funds is the step where otherwise clean Golden Visa files stall. Buyers focus on property selection and lawyer due diligence, then discover the bank or migration office will not accept how money moved. Circular 1/2026 (issued 22 April 2026) made the Greek bank payment trail non-negotiable for permit applications under Law 5100/2024. This guide separates bank KYC from migration proof of funds, lists acceptable source documents, and walks through a €400,000 Crete purchase fund flow.

For AFM and account opening mechanics, start with the [Golden Visa bank account and AFM guide](/guides/greece-golden-visa-bank-account-afm/). For the full acquisition fee stack, see [cost of buying property in Greece](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-greece/).

## Two Layers: Bank KYC vs Migration Proof of Funds

| Layer | Who reviews | What they need | When |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bank KYC | Greek commercial bank | Identity, AFM, source of funds for incoming credits | Account opening and each large transfer |
| Migration proof | Ministry of Migration | Proof full qualifying price paid for declared property | Permit application after deed |

Passing bank compliance does not automatically satisfy migration review, but failing bank compliance prevents building the trail migration requires. Treat both as sequential gates.

---

## Circular 1/2026 Payment Trail Requirements

The migration file expects:

- Greek bank account in the main applicant's name (joint accounts with spouse usually acceptable if lawyer confirms)
- Account statements showing credit of funds from documented sources
- SWIFT or domestic transfer confirmation to seller or notary-controlled payment
- Deed and registry extract matching the paid amount and property address
- No unexplained third-party payers without gift or loan documentation

Direct international wire from a foreign bank to the seller's account, bypassing the buyer's Greek account, is the most common rejection pattern in 2026 files. The [Circular 1/2026 explained guide](/guides/greece-golden-visa-circular-2026-explained/) lists submission checklist items tied to payment proof.

## Source of Funds: Document Packages by Profile

| Buyer profile | Typical documents | Bank follow-up |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Salaried employee | Payslips, employment contract, tax returns, savings statements | Employer verification for large sums |
| Business owner | Company accounts, tax filings, dividend resolutions | UBO chart if paying from corporate account |
| Asset seller | Sale deed, settlement statement, bank credit record | Link sale proceeds to current transfer |
| Inheritance | Probate, inheritance tax clearance, executor letter | Donor death certificate and estate docs |
| Portfolio investor | Broker statements, liquidation confirms | Enhanced due diligence over €500K credits |
| Family gift | Gift deed, donor ID, donor source of funds | Full donor KYC chain |

Documents must be recent, typically within three to six months. Non-Greek or non-English documents need certified translation. Your lawyer aligns the package with both bank compliance and migration formatting.

---

## Opening the Account and Transfer Timing

Recommended sequence:

1. Obtain AFM (in person or via lawyer POA)
2. Open Greek bank account with passport, AFM, proof of address, source of funds summary
3. Transfer purchase funds via SWIFT from home country bank (allow 3–7 business days)
4. Maintain cleared balance through deed date
5. Pay transfer tax at 3.09 percent before notary signing
6. Execute deed with payment from Greek account per lawyer instruction
7. Export statements and SWIFT confirms for migration file

Allow four to six weeks total for steps 1–3 before your target deed date. The [AFM guide](/guides/greece-afm-tax-number-property/) covers tax number timing.

## Worked Example: €400,000 Crete Purchase Fund Flow

| Step | Action | Amount | Document produced |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | SWIFT from UK bank to Greek account | €400,000 | Incoming SWIFT confirm |
| 2 | Bank credits account after KYC | €400,000 | Account statement |
| 3 | Pay transfer tax (3.09%) | €12,360 | Tax payment receipt |
| 4 | Pay seller from Greek account at deed | €400,000 | Transfer confirm + notarial deed |
| 5 | Pay notary and registry fees | ~€8,000 | Fee receipts |
| 6 | Compile migration payment pack | — | Statements + deed + registry |

Total euro need in Greek account before deed: purchase price plus tax and fees, roughly €420,000 to €430,000 on a €400,000 property. Model FX when sending from non-euro accounts.

## What Fails Review: Red Flags

| Red flag | Why it fails | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cash deposit into Greek account | AML prohibition | Use traceable wire only |
| Third-party payer without gift docs | Unknown source | Gift agreement + donor KYC |
| Crypto wallet to seller direct | No fiat trail | Liquidate to bank, document chain |
| Split payments outside account | Incomplete trail | Consolidate through Greek account |
| Last-minute transfer on deed day | Compliance hold risk | Transfer 2–4 weeks early |
| Account in different name | Identity mismatch | Single applicant account or lawyer-approved joint |

## Currency, SWIFT, and FX Documentation

Non-euro buyers should retain:

- FX conversion confirmation from sending bank
- SWIFT MT103 or equivalent with sender and beneficiary details matching passport name
- Intermediate correspondent bank records if funds route through multiple institutions

Splitting €400,000 into four €100,000 tranches is acceptable if statements show cumulative balance meeting the purchase price before deed. Inform your lawyer of split strategy so the migration narrative is coherent.

---

## Corporate and Trust Structures

Purchasing through a foreign company or trust adds layers. Greek migration expects clarity on ultimate beneficial owner. Corporate buyers need:

- Certificate of incorporation and good standing
- Shareholder register showing UBO above 25 percent
- Board resolution authorising purchase and payment
- Bank statements in the entity name with link to qualifying payment

Many Golden Visa investors buy in personal name for simplicity. Structural purchases require specialist counsel early.

---

## Joint Accounts, Spouses, and Payment Naming

Married couples often ask whether one Greek account can receive funds from both spouses. Banks accept joint accounts when both parties complete KYC, but migration files must show the main applicant as investor of record on the deed. If a non-applicant spouse wires funds without documentation, compliance may freeze credits.

Best practice: main applicant account receives the purchase SWIFT; spouse transfer documented as internal family transfer with marriage certificate on file. Your lawyer confirms naming matches passport, deed, and bank account exactly. Character mismatches (middle name omitted, transliteration differences) cause weeks of reconciliation.

## Retaining Records for Renewal and Audit

Keep seven years of:

- SWIFT confirmations and monthly statements
- Source of funds package used at account opening
- Tax payment receipts for transfer tax
- Notarial deed and registry extracts
- Migration submission receipt and permit card copies

Renewal under Circular 1/2026 may request updated proof that the investment remains held and that original payment trail was legitimate. Digital backup in cloud storage accessible to your lawyer speeds renewal preparation.

These are operational patterns, not legal guarantees. Cross-border tax and exchange control advice in the home country runs parallel to Greek requirements.

## Transfer Tax Payment Sequence and Proof

Transfer tax at 3.09 percent of taxable value must be paid before the notary executes the deed. Proof of funds for migration includes both the seller payment and the tax payment trail:

1. Credit Greek account with purchase amount plus tax reserve
2. Pay transfer tax online via Greek tax portal using buyer AFM
3. Obtain tax clearance certificate
4. Notary verifies tax paid before deed
5. Transfer purchase price to seller from same Greek account
6. Archive all receipts in migration bundle

Skipping the tax step blocks the deed even if purchase funds sit ready. Model tax as part of SWIFT amount when sending from abroad. The [transfer tax guide](/guides/greece-property-transfer-tax-fma/) details calculation on objective versus declared value.

## Lawyer Coordination Between Bank and Migration File

Your property lawyer and immigration lawyer (often the same firm) must align narratives:

- Bank sees source of funds at account opening
- Notary sees cleared balance on deed date
- Migration sees deed amount matching account debits

Inconsistency between declared purchase price, account debits, and deed triggers resubmission. Provide both teams the same fund-flow diagram before first SWIFT. Single-firm bundled retainers reduce handoff errors described in the [Golden Visa lawyer complete guide](/guides/golden-visa-lawyer-greece-complete-guide/).
