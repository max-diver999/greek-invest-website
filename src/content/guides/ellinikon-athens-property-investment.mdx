## What Is The Ellinikon: and Why It Matters for Property Investors

The Ellinikon, in Greek, *To Ellinikon*, is not a conventional neighbourhood. It is a state-scale redevelopment: the decommissioned Hellinikon International Airport, which served Athens from 1938 until closure in March 2001, reimagined as a mixed-use coastal city within a city.

Lamda Development holds the concession from the Greek state to develop approximately 6.2 million square metres of land along the Saronic Gulf. The advertised investment envelope runs to €8 billion. By land area and capital committed, it is routinely described as the largest urban regeneration project currently under construction in Europe, larger in scope than comparable waterfront redevelopments in London, Barcelona, or Hamburg.

For property investors, the significance is structural. Greece's residential supply pipeline has been thin for more than a decade. Modern, energy-efficient stock in prime coastal locations is scarce. The Ellinikon concentrates a decade of planned new supply into a single address with professional developer management, branded residential product, and a visible infrastructure calendar. That concentration creates a price premium before the masterplan is complete, which is either the investment thesis or the primary risk, depending on your holding period and return expectations.

The broader Athens Riviera context matters equally. South Athens municipalities, Glyfada, Voula, Vouliagmeni, Alimos, and Elliniko, have seen sustained foreign and domestic demand since 2019. Average asking prices in the Athens South corridor run at approximately €3,200 per square metre and above for renovated and new-build apartments, with branded Ellinikon product commanding a further premium. For how this submarket fits within Attica as a whole, see the [Athens property investment guide](/guides/athens-property-investment-guide/).

## Masterplan Zones: What Is Being Built and Where

Lamda's masterplan divides the 6.2 million square metre site into distinct functional zones. Each zone has a different delivery timeline, buyer appeal, and impact on surrounding residential values. Understanding the map is essential before you commit capital, you are not buying a finished neighbourhood, you are buying a position within a multi-decade build programme.

**Quick answer:** The Ellinikon masterplan combines residential towers and low-rise districts, a metropolitan park larger than New York's Central Park, a marina and coastal front, a business and innovation hub, retail and hospitality, and a casino resort. Residential phases are the most relevant zone for individual investors; park and transport infrastructure are the catalysts for the infrastructure-led price premium narrative.

| Zone | Primary use | Investor relevance | Indicative delivery |
|---|---|---|---|
| Residential districts | Branded apartments, villas, low-rise | Direct purchase target; Golden Visa qualifying stock | 2026–early 2030s (phased) |
| Metropolitan park | Green space, sports, public realm | Amenity premium for adjacent residential; no direct purchase | 2026–2030s |
| Marina & coastal front | Berths, waterfront dining, leisure | Lifestyle anchor; supports premium pricing on sea-facing units | Late 2020s–2030s |
| Business & innovation hub | Offices, conference, INSEAD campus | Employment anchor; long-term demand driver for rentals | Mid–late 2020s |
| Retail & experience | Shopping, dining, cultural venues | Footfall generator; supports resale liquidity narrative | 2026–2030s |
| Casino resort | Integrated resort, hospitality | Flagship amenity; high-profile but long-dated completion | 2030s |

Residential buyers should focus due diligence on three questions: which phase of which residential district you are entering; what park, road, and metro connectivity will exist at your contracted handover date; and how much of the €8 billion capital plan remains dependent on future phases you do not control.

Adjacent Riviera municipalities benefit from Ellinikon spillover even if you do not buy inside the fence. Glyfada and Voula prices already incorporate expectations of improved coastal infrastructure and international visibility. ## Athens South Pricing: Ellinikon vs the Broader Riviera

Ellinikon does not price like a standard Athens South resale flat. Branded developer product inside the masterplan carries a packaging premium: architectural identity, managed common areas, energy ratings, and the regeneration story itself.

**Quick answer:** Broader Athens South averages roughly €3,200 per square metre and above. Ellinikon entry-level branded units typically start at €800,000 total, often at or above €4,500–6,000 per square metre depending on phase, view, and fit-out. You are paying for scarcity of modern coastal product and front-loaded infrastructure expectations.

| Submarket | Typical price band (2026) | €800,000 buys (approx.) | Buyer profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| Athens South average | €3,200+/m² | ~250m² (well above 120m² GV floor) | Value-focused Riviera entry |
| Glyfada new-build | €4,000–5,500/m² | ~145–200m² | Established suburb, immediate lifestyle |
| Voula quality stock | €4,000–5,000/m² | ~160–200m² | Family coastal, lower branding premium |
| Ellinikon entry branded | €800,000+ total | 120–180m² typical configs | Regeneration narrative, off-plan |
| Ellinikon premium seafront | €1.5M–3M+ | Varies by phase | Capital growth, lifestyle flagship |

The table explains why Ellinikon is not the cheapest route to an Attica Golden Visa, it is the most professionally packaged route at the top of the €800K zone. Buyers who need maximum square metres per euro often achieve better arithmetic in Piraeus or lower Glyfada stock. Buyers who want the regeneration address accept that the market has already priced much of the upside into current asking levels.

National context reinforces the selectivity of this market. Foreign real estate acquisition inflows fell 25.3% to €2,055.6 million in 2025 from €2,750.3 million in 2024, as buyers recalibrated to Law 5100/2024 threshold changes. Yet new Golden Visa residence permit approvals rose 95% year-on-year to 8,879 in 2025, a backlog clearing through the system, not evidence that premium-zone demand has disappeared. ## Off-Plan vs Resale: Which Route Fits Ellinikon?

Most Ellinikon residential supply is off-plan or near-completion new-build within Lamda's phased programme. Resale options inside the masterplan will grow as early phases complete and owners circulate units, but the dominant buyer pathway in 2026 remains pre-completion or at-handover purchase from the developer pipeline.

**Quick answer:** Off-plan gives access to Ellinikon at developer pricing with modern specifications, but exposes you to construction delays and staged payments without ownership until deed signing. Resale in neighbouring Riviera suburbs gives immediate keys, established context, and often lower per-square-metre cost, without the same masterplan upside.

| Factor | Ellinikon off-plan | Riviera resale (Glyfada/Voula) |
|---|---|---|
| Price entry | €800,000+ branded minimum | €3,200–5,500/m²; more size per euro |
| VAT / transfer tax | New-build: 3.09% transfer tax if VAT suspension applies | 3.09% transfer tax on resale |
| Inspection | Plans, show apartments, site visits | Physical unit, immediate condition assessment |
| Completion risk | Developer, permit, timeline exposure | None, property exists |
| Customisation | Some finish choices in early phases | Limited to renovation |
| Golden Visa timing | Application after contractual commitment | Application after deed |
| Income start | After handover and furnishing | Immediate if tenant-ready |
| Resale liquidity pre-handover | Thin assignment market | Established secondary market |

The off-plan process in Greece carries specific legal characteristics: ownership transfers only at notarial deed when the certificate of completion is issued. Until then, your position is contractual. For milestone payments, bank guarantees or escrow provisions are the critical protection, not optional extras. The dedicated [off-plan property Greece guide](/guides/off-plan-property-greece-guide/) covers payment stages, developer due diligence, and Golden Visa eligibility mechanics in full.

For buyers comparing off-plan Ellinikon against resale Riviera stock, the decision is rarely about which is objectively better. It is about whether you want exposure to the regeneration equity story at a premium, or established neighbourhood value with immediate utility.

## Infrastructure Timeline: What Delivers When

The infrastructure-led price premium narrative only holds if infrastructure actually arrives. Lamda has brought forward several components ahead of the full masterplan horizon, but large elements remain dated to the late 2020s and 2030s.

**Quick answer:** Residential towers and early park sections are operational or nearing completion in the 2026 window. Marina expansion, full park maturation, business district scale-up, and the casino resort extend into the 2030s. Prices today assume much of this calendar, delay is the central investment risk.

| Infrastructure component | Status (2026) | Full operational horizon | Price impact if delayed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early residential phases | Delivering / near handover | 2026–2028 | Moderate, units exist but amenity gap persists |
| Metropolitan park (core sections) | Under construction / partial open | 2028–2030s | High, park is key lifestyle differentiator |
| Coastal front & public realm | Phased | Late 2020s | Moderate, affects seafront premium |
| Marina | Planning / early works | Late 2020s–2030s | High for marina-view inventory |
| Metro / road connectivity upgrades | Partially complete | 2027–2030 | High, accessibility drives broad demand |
| Business hub & INSEAD campus | Under development | Mid–late 2020s | Moderate, rental demand driver |
| Casino resort | Long-dated | 2030s | Low near-term; flagship long-term amenity |

Investors should map their holding period against this calendar explicitly. A buyer planning a ten-year hold can absorb delay risk more comfortably than a buyer expecting to crystallise gain on handover in 2027. Construction slippage of twelve to twenty-four months beyond contracted dates is not unusual in large Mediterranean regeneration projects, Ellinikon is not immune simply because of its scale and state backing.

## Golden Visa Fit: €800K, 120m², and the No-STR Rule

Ellinikon sits entirely within the Attica prime zone under Law 5100/2024. Every qualifying residential purchase in the district requires a minimum €800,000 investment in a single property with at least 120 square metres of usable area.

**Quick answer:** Ellinikon qualifies for the Golden Visa without threshold ambiguity, entry units already price at or above €800,000. The 120m² rule is typically satisfied by standard configurations at entry level. Short-term tourist rentals on the qualifying asset are prohibited; long-term leases of twelve months or more are allowed.

| Golden Visa requirement | Ellinikon implication |
|---|---|
| €800,000 minimum (Attica prime zone) | Entry branded units start at threshold, no sub-threshold ambiguity |
| 120m² usable area minimum | Typical entry configs clear floor; verify on deed plans |
| Single property rule | One qualifying asset; no portfolio stacking |
| No short-term rental on qualifying asset | Airbnb / Booking.com prohibited on GV property |
| Long-term rental permitted | Standard income route; Riviera gross yields often 3.0–3.8% |
| Application timing (off-plan) | File after contractual commitment and consideration paid |

The no-short-term-rental restriction matters for Ellinikon more than for some Athens submarkets because coastal buyers often assume holiday-rental income. That assumption is incorrect for the Golden Visa qualifying property. If rental income is part of your model, it must be long-term residential, and yield expectations should be set accordingly.

## Risks: Construction Delay, Premium Pricing, and Market Selectivity

Every regeneration investment carries a gap between narrative and delivery. Ellinikon amplifies that gap because of scale.

**Construction and infrastructure delay.** Large urban regeneration projects routinely slip beyond initial public timelines. If park sections, marina works, or transport links delay by two to three years, buyers who paid an infrastructure premium may see near-term price stagnation while adjacent completed suburbs continue to trade on established amenity.

**Premium pricing.** Entry at €800,000 or above is not a discount to the market, it is the market. Buyers entering in 2026 are not buying pre-narrative pricing from 2018. Due diligence should stress-test whether your return model still works if annual appreciation moderates to the Bank of Greece's base case of continued but slower growth.

**Pre-handover liquidity.** Assigning an off-plan contract before deed signing is possible in some developer structures but remains thin in Greece relative to Spain or the UAE. If your exit plan requires selling before completion, confirm assignment rights in the preliminary agreement before paying deposits.

**Golden Visa regulatory risk.** Thresholds changed materially in 2024. Further amendments are always possible. The qualifying property's no-STR rule is actively enforced in programme compliance. Treat regulatory stability as an assumption to monitor, not a permanent guarantee.

**Developer concentration.** Lamda Development is the dominant counterparty for on-site residential product. Developer due diligence, financial standing, permit status, escrow provisions, is non-negotiable. The [due diligence guide for Greece property](/guides/due-diligence-greece-property/) applies with heightened rigour here.

## Closing Verification Checklist

Before committing to an Ellinikon purchase, off-plan or resale, verify the following:

1. **Zone and phase**: confirm which masterplan district and delivery phase your unit belongs to, and what infrastructure is contracted versus aspirational at handover.
2. **Golden Visa arithmetic**: €800,000 minimum, 120m² usable area on a single residential asset, no short-term rental on the qualifying property.
3. **Payment protection**: bank guarantee or escrow for all pre-deed stage payments on off-plan contracts.
4. **Timeline stress test**: model returns assuming twelve to twenty-four months of delay beyond the developer's stated handover date.
5. **Yield assumptions**: long-term residential only on the GV asset; use 3.0–3.8% gross for Riviera stock unless you have verified local comparables.
6. **Total cost stack**: purchase price plus 7–10% acquisition costs, annual ENFIA, insurance, and management fees.
7. **Independent counsel**: Greek lawyer review of the preliminary agreement before any deposit; engineer survey at handover for completed stock.

Ellinikon is the most ambitious property story in Greece today. For the right buyer, long horizon, Golden Visa or lifestyle motivation, comfortable with premium pricing and phased delivery, it is a coherent investment. For the wrong buyer, yield-first, short hold, Airbnb-dependent, it is an expensive mismatch. The distinction is planning, not marketing.
