Legal costs are consistently underestimated by Golden Visa buyers. The [total cost of buying property in Greece](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-greece/) article covers the full 7–10% acquisition fee stack; this guide focuses specifically on what lawyers charge, what they do for that money, and how to avoid costly mistakes when instructing counsel.

## What a Lawyer Does at Each Stage of the Golden Visa Purchase

The Greek property buying process has five legal checkpoints where professional representation prevents irreversible mistakes. For Golden Visa buyers, the stakes at each checkpoint are higher — an error does not just delay a property purchase, it can disqualify the investment from the visa programme entirely.

| Stage | Legal Work | Typical Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-purchase due diligence | Title search, encumbrance check, ENFIA clearance, tier verification | Before preliminary agreement |
| Preliminary agreement | Review or draft symvolaio proanaforas, advise on deposit terms | Week 2–4 |
| Tax registration | Obtain Greek Tax Number (AFM) for buyer | Parallel with above |
| Final deed | Attend notarial signing, confirm FMA payment, receive certified copies | 4–8 weeks after preliminary |
| Land Registry and GV application | Register transfer, compile investment file for Ministry of Migration | 1–6 weeks post-deed |

**Title search.** A Greek title chain must be clean for 20 years. The lawyer reviews Land Registry records for mortgages, easements, seizures, and any contested ownership — including inheritance-derived complications, which are common in Greek property due to the country's intestate succession rules. A property with unresolved inheritance claims cannot be used for a Golden Visa application.

**Tier verification.** Since the March 2023 circular, Greece operates a two-tier investment threshold: €800,000 for Attica, Thessaloniki, Mykonos, Santorini, and islands with populations above 3,100; €400,000 everywhere else. Your lawyer must confirm which tier applies before you commit. The [Golden Visa property tiers guide](/guides/greece-golden-visa-property-tiers-2026/) details the zone boundaries; the legal check confirms your specific cadastral entry matches the right tier. Getting this wrong means the investment does not qualify.

**Application filing.** Once the deed is registered, your immigration lawyer compiles the Ministry of Migration package: authenticated purchase deed, Land Registry certificate, health insurance proof, passport copies, and the investment compliance declaration. They submit to the relevant Migration Department, attend the biometric appointment with you (or as your representative under power of attorney), and track the file through to permit issuance. See the [full Golden Visa timeline](/guides/greece-golden-visa-timeline-application-2026/) for processing stages and current wait times.

## Fee Structures: Percentage vs Fixed Retainer

Greek lawyers quote Golden Visa work in two main structures. Understanding the difference prevents misaligned expectations — and underbudgeting.

| Fee Structure | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Percentage of purchase price | 1–1.5% of the agreed purchase price | Standard single-property purchases |
| Fixed retainer (property law) | €3,000–€6,000 flat | High-value properties where percentage would be disproportionate |
| Fixed retainer (immigration) | €1,500–€4,000 flat | Immigration filing, billed separately from property work |
| Bundled package | Single fee covering both property and immigration | Common at Athens boutique firms, typically 15–20% cheaper than split |

**Percentage-based fees** are the most common for property work. The percentage does not drop proportionally for higher values — a €800,000 property at 1% generates €8,000 in fees, but most lawyers charge at least 1% with a floor of around €4,000, meaning sub-€400,000 purchases may see effective rates closer to 1.5%. Confirm whether the percentage applies to the purchase price or the objective value; it should be the purchase price.

**Fixed immigration retainers** cover Ministry filing, biometric attendance, and permit collection. This is billed separately from property legal work at most firms. Rates vary significantly between Athens-based firms with dedicated Golden Visa departments (€1,500–€2,500) and generalist provincial lawyers who handle fewer cases annually (€2,500–€4,000). The [common Golden Visa mistakes guide](/guides/greece-golden-visa-mistakes-avoid/) covers the risks of using lawyers without specialist GV experience.

## Immigration Lawyer vs Property Lawyer: What Changes

Some buyers attempt to use a property-only lawyer for both roles, or hire an immigration-only firm unfamiliar with property law. Both approaches create gaps.

| Role | Core Skills | What They Cannot Do |
|---|---|---|
| Property lawyer | Title search, deed preparation, Land Registry | Navigate Ministry of Migration systems efficiently |
| Immigration lawyer | Residency permit filing, biometric scheduling, permit tracking | Conduct title searches, review preliminary agreements |
| Dual-qualified / boutique GV firm | Both above | Nothing — this is the optimal structure for GV buyers |

**Property lawyers without immigration experience** tend to underestimate Ministry of Migration processing quirks — document authentication chains, handling of non-EU buyer passport types, and the specific Land Registry certificate formats the Ministry accepts. Errors here can cause application rejections that are fixable but add months to the process.

**Immigration lawyers without property experience** miss title search red flags. A clean immigration filing means nothing if the underlying property has an unregistered easement or an inheritance dispute. The [foreign buyer guide](/guides/buy-property-greece-foreigner/) walks through the full purchase sequence; a lawyer who does not understand each step cannot protect you across it.

**Dual-qualified or boutique GV firms** charge a bundle fee that is usually 15–20% below the combined cost of two specialists. They are most concentrated in Athens and Thessaloniki. For Crete or Peloponnese properties, check whether the firm has a local Land Registry correspondent — some Athens firms sub-contract rural title work at additional cost.

## Family Application Costs: Worked Examples

Adding dependents to a Greece Golden Visa is straightforward in principle but adds measurable legal cost per person.

| Applicant Composition | Government Fees | Legal Fees (estimate) | Total Legal + Gov |
|---|---|---|---|
| Main applicant only | €2,000 (investment application) + €500 (permit) | €5,500–€10,000 | €8,000–€12,500 |
| Couple (main + spouse) | + €150 biometric per spouse | + €500–€1,500 | + €650–€1,650 |
| Family of four (couple + 2 children under 21) | + €300 biometric (2 children) | + €1,000–€3,000 | + €1,300–€3,300 |
| Adult child (21–24, student) | + €150 biometric | + €600–€1,500 (extra docs) | + €750–€1,650 |

Government fees are fixed; legal fees vary by firm and complexity. A family of four on a €400,000 property should budget approximately €9,000–€15,000 in total legal and government charges, excluding the property purchase price and standard acquisition costs (FMA, notary, Land Registry).

**Power of attorney considerations.** If you cannot be in Greece for the deed signing or biometric appointment, your lawyer acts under a notarised power of attorney. Preparing, authenticating, and apostilling a PoA from outside Greece costs €300–€600 and requires extra lead time — factor this into your timeline.

## Renewal Legal Fees: Year Five and Beyond

A Greece Golden Visa is issued for five years and is renewable indefinitely provided the investment is maintained. The [renewal requirements guide](/guides/greece-golden-visa-renewal-requirements/) covers eligibility; this section focuses on legal cost.

Renewal is significantly cheaper than the initial application because the title and immigration due diligence are mostly carry-forward. The lawyer's work reduces to:

- Confirming ongoing ownership (Land Registry check)
- Obtaining an updated ENFIA clearance certificate
- Preparing the investment compliance declaration
- Submitting renewal paperwork and attending (or representing at) the biometric reappointment

Typical renewal legal fees: **€800–€2,000 for the main applicant**, plus **€300–€600 per dependent**. For a family of four, total renewal legal cost runs €1,700–€3,800. Government fees for renewal are €500 per main applicant plus €150 per dependent biometric.

One practical point: if you switch lawyers between the initial application and renewal, allow extra time for the new firm to review the existing file. The handover adds €300–€800 in file review fees at most firms.

## Red Flags When Choosing Golden Visa Counsel

The Greece Golden Visa industry has attracted a number of generalist or opportunistic legal services alongside the established specialists. Knowing what to check avoids costly mistakes.

**Verify bar registration.** Every Greek lawyer must be registered with their regional bar association. Athens Bar Association (DSA) registration can be confirmed at dsa.gr. Ask for the registration number and check it before signing anything. Unregistered legal consultants cannot appear before Greek courts or notaries on your behalf.

**Insist on an engagement letter.** The engagement letter should specify the scope of services, which fees cover which stages, the instalment schedule, what happens if the deal falls through, and who handles the immigration filing. A lawyer who resists providing a written scope is a lawyer who wants flexibility to charge extra later.

**Avoid commission-linked arrangements.** Some property agencies in Greece offer to recommend a lawyer who then returns a referral fee to the agent. This is not illegal but creates a direct conflict of interest. Your lawyer should be accountable to you, not to the person selling you the property. Choosing independent counsel is covered as a core principle in the [mistakes to avoid guide](/guides/greece-golden-visa-mistakes-avoid/).

**Fee below €1,500 for a full GV transaction is a warning sign.** A properly conducted title search, preliminary agreement review, deed attendance, Land Registry registration, and immigration filing cannot realistically be delivered below €1,500 per engagement — the disbursements alone (Land Registry searches, notarised copies, Ministry fees payable by the lawyer on your behalf) consume a significant portion of that. Fees quoted well below market usually mean steps will be skipped.

**Promises of guaranteed approval.** No lawyer can guarantee Ministry of Migration approval. What they can guarantee is a correctly compiled, complete application. Any firm promising visa approval as part of the retainer is misrepresenting what legal services can deliver.
