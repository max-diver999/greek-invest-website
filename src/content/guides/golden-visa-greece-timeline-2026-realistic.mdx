## Three Scenarios: Best, Typical, and Problem Files

Greece Golden Visa timelines in 2026 typically range from twelve months for clean single-applicant resale files to fifteen months for family submissions and eighteen to twenty-four months when title or Circular 1/2026 documentation gaps trigger resubmission, with Greece approving 8,879 permits in 2025 while roughly 11,553 applications remained pending. MORE Group plans fifteen months as the default relocation assumption.

| Scenario | Total months | Purchase phase | Permit phase | Profile |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Best case | 12 | 8 weeks | 8 months | Clean resale, single applicant, Athens lawyer, complete Circular 1/2026 file |
| Typical | 15 | 10 weeks | 11 months | Family of four, regional €400K property, standard backlog |
| Problem file | 18 to 24 | 16 to 24 weeks | 14 to 18 months | Title legalisation, wrong tier corrected late, bank trail gaps |

Greece approved 8,879 Golden Visa permits in 2025, a 95 percent year-on-year increase, while approximately 11,553 applications remained pending entering 2026. Higher throughput did not eliminate queue time. New filings continue to arrive faster than historical averages, so conservative planning beats optimistic marketing.

## Month-by-Month Calendar From Offer to Permit Card

A realistic Greece Golden Visa calendar spans months one to two for lawyer engagement and offer, months two to four for due diligence and deed registration, months four to five for biometrics, and months five to fifteen for Ministry permit processing under Circular 1/2026 rules requiring filing within sixty days of registration. MORE Group aligns AFM, Greek banking, and apostille workstreams in parallel from month one.

### Months 1 to 2: Search, lawyer, and offer

- Instruct independent Greek lawyer before deposit
- Verify Golden Visa tier, 120 square metre usable area, single deed
- Sign preliminary agreement and pay deposit (typically 10 percent)
- Open AFM and Greek bank account in parallel

Link: [making an offer on Greek property](/guides/making-offer-greek-property-step-by-step/) and [buying as a foreigner](/guides/buy-property-greece-foreigner/).

### Months 2 to 4: Due diligence and deed

- Title search, cadastre check, ENFIA clearance, engineer certificate if needed
- Pay 3.09 percent transfer tax
- Sign final notarial deed and register at land registry
- File permit application within 60 days of registration (Circular 1/2026)

### Months 4 to 5: Biometrics and bridging document

- All family members attend biometric appointments in Greece
- Receive application receipt or bridging confirmation
- Maintain valid health insurance for every applicant

### Months 5 to 15: Permit processing queue

- Ministry of Migration reviews investment compliance
- Authorities may request supplemental bank transfer proof or updated registry extract
- Five-year residence permit card issued upon approval

## What Adds Months: Delay Drivers Ranked

Incomplete Circular 1/2026 checklists typically add two to four months, wrong tier or 120 square metre failures add two to six months, and missing the sixty-day post-registration filing window adds one to three months to Greece Golden Visa timelines in 2026. MORE Group ranks bank transfer proof gaps and missing apostilles as the most preventable delay drivers on family files.

| Delay driver | Typical slip | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Incomplete Circular 1/2026 checklist | 2 to 4 months | Use immigration lawyer checklist before submission |
| Payment not through Greek account | 1 to 3 months | Route full price via Greek bank before deed |
| 120 m² or tier error discovered late | 2 to 6 months | Lawyer verifies before preliminary agreement |
| Mortgage or encumbrance on title | 1 to 4 months | Full title search before deposit |
| Missing apostille on family docs | 3 to 8 weeks | Start consular legalisation at offer stage |
| Missing 60-day filing window | 1 to 3 months | Book appointment when deed date is known |
| Border zone approval required | 3 to 9 months | Check zone map before viewing |

## Family Applications: Where Time Multiplies

Single applicants with one lawyer and one biometric appointment sit at the fast end of the typical band. Families with four parents, two children, and two adults may hold eight permits from one investment but must assemble eight document sets, eight insurance policies, and eight biometric slots.

| Family size | Document assembly | Biometric scheduling | Typical permit slip vs single |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 applicant | 2 to 3 weeks | 1 day | Baseline |
| 2 adults | 4 to 5 weeks | 1 to 2 days | +2 to 4 weeks |
| 2 adults + 2 children | 6 to 8 weeks | 2 to 3 days | +4 to 6 weeks |
| Extended family (8 permits) | 10 to 14 weeks | 3 to 5 days | +6 to 10 weeks |

Submitting all members together still beats staggered dependent applications because property documentation is compiled once.

## Remote Buyers and Power of Attorney

Remote Golden Visa purchase requires power of attorney notarisation and apostille in one to three weeks, then AFM and Greek bank account setup before deed signing, with biometrics still requiring in-person attendance in Greece for most family members after registration. MORE Group red flag: finding property first and opening banking in the final week routinely adds four to eight weeks.

Remote purchase is standard but timing fails when administrative steps are sequenced wrong. Correct order:

1. Grant POA to Greek lawyer (allow 1 to 3 weeks for notarisation and apostille)
2. Obtain AFM via lawyer
3. Open Greek bank account
4. Lawyer signs preliminary agreement and deed on buyer's behalf
5. Buyer travels for biometrics after registration

Buyers who find property first and treat AFM and banking as afterthoughts often lose four to eight weeks between deed target and actual signing. The [Golden Visa lawyer guide](/guides/golden-visa-lawyer-greece-complete-guide/) covers POA scope limits.

**Insider tip:** MORE Group schedules deed registration and biometric appointments in the same travel week whenever possible; splitting those trips adds three to six weeks of calendar slip even when the Ministry queue itself is unchanged.

## What You Can Do in Greece While Waiting

Golden Visa applicants with accepted files may obtain bridging confirmation allowing legal stay in Greece during Ministry processing, but employment remains restricted and Schengen travel outside Greece requires lawyer confirmation of document validity before the five-year card arrives. MORE Group advises long-term leasing a non-qualifying property if immediate housing is needed.

After biometrics and while the file is pending:

- Long-term lease a non-GV property if you need immediate housing (qualifying asset STR still banned)
- Enrol children in schools if bridging status allows legal stay
- Open utility accounts and register with local municipality where permitted
- Do not begin employment without appropriate work authorisation

Schengen travel beyond Greece before card issuance requires lawyer confirmation of which document the holder carries. Tourist visa days do not extend automatically because you own property or have a pending application.

Heritage or commercial conversion projects at the €250,000 tier add planning permits and construction phases measured in years, not months.

Buyers choosing off-plan for price reasons should separate property delivery timeline from residency timeline in financial models. The permit clock starts only when the investment qualifies under registered title rules.

## When to Escalate Timeline Risk

Escalate to specialist counsel if any of the following appear during due diligence: inheritance shares among multiple Greek heirs, building constructed before 1983 without final permit, seller is a company in liquidation, property sits within 500 metres of a border zone boundary, or bank compliance requests enhanced due diligence beyond standard source documents. Each condition adds a decision point: proceed with conditions precedent in the preliminary agreement, renegotiate price, or walk away before deposit forfeiture.

Buyers comparing Greece with Portugal or Cyprus residency routes should factor timeline alongside capital. Greece's 2026 backlog is longer than some marketing materials suggest but shorter than first-generation post-2024 tier confusion created. Conservative planning at fifteen months total protects family relocation decisions, school enrolment timing, and lease termination dates in the home country. Add two weeks buffer for public holidays affecting notary and migration office hours.

## Bridging Document vs Card: Travel Planning Matrix

Golden Visa travel rights depend on document stage: tourist entry allows only Schengen 90/180 days, submitted applications may allow Greece stay under bridging confirmation with lawyer verification, and five-year cards unlock full residency stay norms. MORE Group confirms which row applies to every family member before school or lease commitments.

| Document stage | Stay in Greece | Schengen short trips | Work in Greece |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tourist entry only | Visa days only | Schengen 90/180 rule | Not permitted |
| Application submitted | Lawyer confirms bridging scope | Limited until card | Not permitted |
| Five-year card issued | Full residency stay | Long-stay norms apply | Separate work permit needed |

Families moving parents should not cancel home-country leases until the lawyer confirms which row applies to every family member. Parent mobility rules mirror the main applicant once cards issue; during processing, elderly parents may face tighter entry conditions than working-age adults.

## Document Expiry Windows That Reset the Clock

Golden Visa supporting documents carry short validity windows that reset timelines when expired: criminal records last three to six months, health insurance must cover biometrics dates, and bank statements often require refresh within thirty to ninety days of filing. MORE Group starts criminal record and apostille work at offer stage for family files.

Several documents carry hidden expiry rules that push problem files past eighteen months:

| Document | Validity window | What happens if expired |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Criminal record certificate | 3 to 6 months from issue | New certificate and apostille |
| Health insurance policy | Must cover biometrics date | Rebook appointment |
| Passport | 6+ months beyond appointment | Renewal before biometrics |
| Marriage certificate (apostilled) | 6 months in some offices | Re-apostille |
| Bank statements for migration | Often 30 to 90 days | Fresh export before filing |

Start criminal record and apostille work at offer stage, not after deed registration. A family of eight with staggered document ages is a common reason typical fifteen-month projections slip to twenty months without any title defect.
