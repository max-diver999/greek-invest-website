Greece designates certain areas as restricted zones where non-EU nationals, including Turkish citizens, require Ministry of National Defense approval before completing a purchase. These designations reflect national security policy, not economic discrimination against Turkish buyers specifically.

Commonly affected areas include islands close to the Turkish coast (parts of Lesbos, Chios, Samos, Kos, and sections of Rhodes) and mainland regions within defined distances of land borders. The exact community-level classification is not always publicly listed in accessible format.

Processing takes 3 to 6 months on average, sometimes longer. EU buyers are exempt. Turkish buyers are not.

**Practical guidance:** If your property search includes any eastern Aegean island, instruct your lawyer to check zone status before viewing, not after making an offer. If approval is required, structure the preliminary agreement with a condition precedent and avoid non-refundable deposits. Thessaloniki, Athens, Halkidiki, Crete, and the Peloponnese are generally unrestricted.
