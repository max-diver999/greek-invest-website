## Pros and Cons: Hungary Guest Investor vs Greece Golden Visa

### Hungary — advantages

- Lower published entry near €250,000 equivalent through approved fund structures
- Ten-year residence permit headline on qualifying Guest Investor subscriptions
- Schengen EU access from Central European hub with lower living costs than Athens Riviera
- Fund route avoids engineer certificates, ENFIA, and Greek notary timelines on day one

### Hungary — disadvantages

- No direct apartment deed on the standard Guest Investor route
- Fund liquidity subject to lock-ups, fees, and manager discretion — not open-market property sale
- Budapest market exposure is indirect unless you buy separately outside the programme
- Family dependent rules require careful verification versus Greece four-parent inclusion

### Greece — advantages

- Tangible Mediterranean property with notarial title and transparent resale comparables
- €400,000 regional tier across Crete, Peloponnese, and dozens of municipalities
- Parents of both spouses included without additional investment capital
- Published Golden Visa law and Circular 1/2026 payment rules banks already recognise

### Greece — disadvantages

- Higher standard residential entry at €400,000 versus Hungary fund headline
- Twelve to eighteen month Ministry processing after deed registration
- Short-term rental prohibited on qualifying Golden Visa unit
- Five-year renewable permit cycle versus Hungary ten-year headline duration

## Red Flags When Choosing Fund vs Deed

**Hungary red flags:** unapproved fund marketing Guest Investor eligibility; prospectus fees unclear over ten years; redemption terms that trap capital beyond your residency needs.

**Greece red flags:** heritage or conversion route sold without permit proof; usable area shortfall on standard 120 square metre tier; payment trail not matching Circular 1/2026.

**Insider tip:** If your wealth team prefers **fund liquidity** and Central Europe connectivity, model Hungary first. If your heirs expect a **deed in the Mediterranean**, Greece remains the clearer asset.

## Decision Framework: Buyer Scenarios

**Scenario A — €260,000 deployable, Schengen mobility only:** Hungary Guest Investor fund may suffice without Mediterranean property ownership.

**Scenario B — family with four parents, one capital pool:** Greece property route typically absorbs the whole household on one investment.

**Scenario D — exit in three to five years:** Greek property open-market sale versus fund redemption schedule — stress-test both before subscribing.

## Fund Subscription vs Notary Deed: Operational Sequence

**Hungary Guest Investor sequence:** select approved fund; complete AML/KYC with fund administrator; wire subscription; receive investment confirmation; file residence application with immigration authority; collect biometric card; maintain qualifying investment for programme duration; track fund reports for renewal evidence.

**Greece Golden Visa sequence:** lawyer title review; deposit contract; notary deed; land registry; assemble Ministry dossier with engineer certificate and payment proofs; biometrics after approval.

Hungary may feel faster to first residence card when fund compliance is clean. Greece delivers tangible asset immediately on deed day even while permit queue runs. Investors who need **something to show heirs** often accept Greek timeline friction. Investors optimising **entry capital** alone may accept fund opacity.

Review [cheapest Golden Visa routes](/guides/cheapest-golden-visa-property-greece-2026/) including €250,000 conversion and heritage tracks before assuming Hungary is the only sub-€400,000 Schengen path with Greek real estate characteristics.

Central Europe fund routes also mean **currency and manager risk** distinct from Greek land registry risk. A Budapest fund unit can lose value while your residence remains valid if you stay invested per programme rules. A Crete villa can fall in market price while still satisfying Golden Visa retention if you maintain the deed. Stress-test both risk types with your wealth adviser, not only immigration marketing slides.

Hungary’s Guest Investor relaunch attracted attention from buyers who previously looked at Portugal funds after real estate ended there. Greece still offers what Portugal no longer does on standard routes: a **named property** on your balance sheet. If heir transparency matters, ask whether your children can inherit a fund unit as easily as a Greek deed — succession law answers differ.

Renewal evidence differs: Greece renewals centre on retained property and clean tax certificates; Hungary renewals require proof the fund investment still meets Guest Investor definitions after any fund merger or restructuring. Read fund prospectus change-of-control clauses before you treat ten-year residence as set-and-forget.

Language integration for future citizenship also diverges: Greek naturalisation tests require Greek proficiency after years of physical presence; Hungary sets its own integration standards on a separate track from Guest Investor residence. Neither programme sells passports, but heirs inheriting a Greek deed face clearer land registry rules than heirs parsing fund unit transfers in Budapest vehicles. Compare annual fund management fees against Greek ENFIA and maintenance before you optimise on entry price alone. A five-year holding cost table from your lawyer beats any single headline minimum quoted at an immigration fair for your family size.

## Due Diligence Comparison: Fund Subscription vs Greek Notary Deed

Hungary due diligence starts with fund prospectus review: approved Guest Investor eligibility, management fees over ten years, redemption penalties, and AML/KYC with the fund administrator. Immigration counsel files residence evidence after subscription confirmation. There is no engineer certificate or land registry deed on the standard route.

Greece due diligence follows property law: title chain, cadastre encumbrances, engineer usable-area report, ENFIA clearance, and Circular 1/2026 payment trail before notary appointment. Ministry dossier assembly follows deed registration.

| Checkpoint | Hungary Guest Investor | Greece Golden Visa property |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Qualifying investment | Approved fund units | Residential deed |
| Third-party verification | Fund administrator KYC | Lawyer + engineer |
| Size compliance | Not applicable | 120 m² usable on standard tiers |
| Immigration evidence | Subscription certificate | Deed + payment proofs |
| STR on qualifying asset | N/A | Prohibited on GV unit |
| Renewal risk | Fund rule changes | Property retention + taxes |
