## Why the Standard "7 to 10%" Figure Understates Real Costs


Every reputable guide to Greek property mentions the 7 to 10% acquisition cost rule. The figure is accurate as far as it goes, it captures FMA transfer tax (3.09%), notary fees (0.8 to 1.2%), lawyer fees (1 to 1.5%), land registry (0.475 to 0.65%), and agent commission (2 to 2.5%). What it does not capture is a second layer of transaction costs that are either calculated on a different base, triggered by specific property or buyer characteristics, or accrue in the months after completion.

Buyers who arrive in Greece budgeting precisely 8% above the asking price regularly find themselves 2 to 3% short by the time keys are handed over. The shortfall is not negligible: on a €500,000 property, 2% represents €10,000, enough to create real financing stress if not anticipated.

Land registry fees are part of the standard 7 to 10% stack (0.475 to 0.65% of objective value). Your notary or lawyer should itemise this separately; if it appears buried in their fees, ask for the breakdown.
