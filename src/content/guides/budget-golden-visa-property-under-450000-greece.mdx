## The Three Tiers and What Fits a €450K Cap

Law 5100/2024 defines three investment thresholds. Only one aligns cleanly with a €450,000 total-capital ceiling for most buyers.

| Tier | Minimum Investment | Fits €450K All-In? | Typical Buyer Fit |
|---|---|---|---|

€800,000 prime zone purchases in Athens, Thessaloniki, Mykonos or Santorini exceed a €450K cap by definition. Do not anchor search there if total capital is fixed.

€250,000 conversion route has the lowest headline price but rarely fits €450K all-in. A €250,000 commercial shell plus €80,000 to €150,000 conversion, plus 7 to 10 percent fees, often lands at €370,000 to €440,000 before furnishing, with 12 to 36 month project risk. Mentioned for completeness; this guide focuses the €400,000 regional tier where finished stock and predictable timelines dominate.

## Law 5100/2024 Constraints That Affect Budget Search

Three rules eliminate large segments of "cheap" listings from a Golden Visa search, regardless of price.

120m² single-deed minimum. Standard residential tiers require one property with at least 120 square metres usable area. A €350,000 two-bedroom of 95m² fails even though price is attractive. Budget buyers must filter for size first, then price.

Short-term rental ban. The qualifying property cannot hold an STR licence while the Golden Visa is active. Do not model Airbnb income to "fund" the fee stack on the same deed.

Single asset, one title. Combining two studios on separate deeds does not work unless legally merged pre-application.

Greece granted 8,879 Golden Visa approvals in 2025, confirming sustained demand. Budget buyers compete for compliant regional stock; early due diligence matters.

Understanding fees prevents the €450K cap breach.

## Red Flags That Blow a €450K Budget

Hidden renovation need. "Light refresh" on older Peloponnese stone houses can exceed €40,000. Survey before deposit.

Cadastral undervaluation assumed in marketing. Transfer tax uses cadastral value, but if cadastral revaluation jumps, tax bill rises.

Unlicensed extensions. Legalisation fines and engineer fees are not in the standard 7 to 10 percent stack.

Buying at €420,000 with no fee quote. All-in exceeds €450,000 at upper fee range.

Two-property bundling pitches. Does not satisfy 120m² single-deed rule.

STR yield promises. Illegal on qualifying asset; income cannot subsidise fees as modelled.

Off-plan without permit issued. Delays add carrying costs (insurance, taxes) that erode contingency.

Border-zone listings marketed as "cheap." Ministry of Defence approval adds months and may fail; not a budget shortcut.

## Application Timeline and Cash Flow Planning

Budget planning must include when capital leaves your account, not only how much.

Months 0-2: Due diligence retainer, deposit 10% on preliminary agreement (often €35,000 to €40,000 on €400K).

Months 2-4: Balance purchase funds through Greek bank account, transfer tax and notary at closing.

Months 4-5: Golden Visa application fees, insurance, biometrics travel if required.

Months 5-18: Carry ENFIA, utilities, insurance while permit processes. Budget €3,000 to €6,000 annual carrying cost depending on size and pool.

Greece's 8,879 approvals in 2025 did not eliminate queue time. Do not assume permit in hand within six months of purchase.

## FX and Non-Euro Savings: Budget Impact on €450K Cap

Buyers funding from USD, GBP, AED, or other non-euro accounts must model conversion spread inside the €450,000 ceiling, not only Greek acquisition fees. A €400,000 purchase with 8 percent fees already consumes €432,000. A 2 percent adverse FX move on a $470,000 equivalent transfer adds roughly €8,000 to effective cost before the deed clears.

| Funding currency | Typical FX friction | €450K cap risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| USD | 1.5 to 2.5% spread + wire | High on tight €400K purchase | Forward contract or staged transfer after lawyer quote |
| GBP | 1 to 2% spread | Medium | Transfer when rate locks within budget model |
| AED / SAR | Bank corridor fees | Medium | Confirm Greek bank IBAN before large SWIFT |
| EUR (EU bank) | Minimal | Low | Focus fee stack only |

Practical rule: If your hard cap is €450,000 all-in, target €395,000 purchase price when funding from volatile currencies, or hold 3 percent FX buffer on top of the 8 percent fee stack. Payment trail rules in the [bank transfer proof of funds guide](/guides/golden-visa-greece-bank-transfer-proof-funds/) apply equally to budget buyers; a compliance hold that delays deed day adds carrying costs that erode the same contingency you reserved for furnishing.

Regional villas and large apartments often sell unfurnished. Furnishing a 180 square metre home for long-term rental or family use typically runs €15,000 to €35,000 depending on kitchen specification and climate systems.

| Line item | Low estimate | High estimate | Counts toward GV minimum? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kitchen appliances | €3,000 | €8,000 | No (not part of €400K deed) |
| Beds, sofas, dining | €4,000 | €10,000 | No |
| HVAC or pool equipment | €2,000 | €12,000 | No |
| LTR tenant package | €6,000 | €15,000 | No |

These costs sit outside the qualifying purchase price but inside your personal €450,000 deployable capital if you treat the cap as total project budget. Buyers who spend €440,000 all-in on deed and fees with only €10,000 left may need to phase furnishing over two years while the property sits partially empty.
