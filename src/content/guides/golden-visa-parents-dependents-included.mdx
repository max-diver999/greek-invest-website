Multigenerational families drive a growing share of Greece Golden Visa applications. Law 5100/2024 and Circular 1/2026 (issued 22 April 2026) explicitly include parents in the family unit, a provision that distinguishes Greece from many EU residency programmes limited to spouse and minor children. For investors from the Middle East, South Asia, and East Asia, bringing retired parents to Mediterranean healthcare and Schengen access is often the primary motivation alongside the property asset itself.

This guide focuses on the parent-specific angle: who qualifies, what documents elderly dependents need, how costs scale, how biometrics work for older applicants, and how renewal and citizenship rules differ from the general family framework covered in the [Greece Golden Visa family members guide](/guides/greece-golden-visa-family-members-rules/).

---

## Which Parents Qualify and How Many Permits You Can Hold

The family definition under Law 5100/2024 covers four parent categories:

| Parent category | Qualifying link | Age or dependency test |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Investor's mother | Birth certificate | None |
| Investor's father | Birth certificate | None |
| Spouse's mother | Marriage certificate + spouse birth cert | None |
| Spouse's father | Marriage certificate + spouse birth cert | None |

A household with two adults, two minor children, and four living parents can therefore hold up to eight permits from one €400,000 Crete purchase or one €800,000 Athens purchase. The investment threshold does not rise with family size. Only administrative fees scale per person.

Parents are permit holders, not co-investors. They hold no ownership stake in the qualifying property unless separately added to the title deed for estate planning reasons, which is uncommon and requires distinct legal advice.

## No Extra Investment: What Actually Costs Money

The qualifying property price is identical whether you apply alone or with six dependents:

| Tier | Minimum investment | Where it applies |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Regional | €400,000 | Crete, Peloponnese, Rhodes (non-restricted), most islands |
| Prime | €800,000 | Attica, Thessaloniki, Mykonos, Santorini, large islands |
| Conversion | €250,000 | Heritage or commercial conversion only |

Per-person costs for parents at application and each five-year renewal:

| Cost item | Typical range per parent | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Government biometric fee | ~€150 | Paid at appointment |
| Immigration lawyer | €500–€1,500 | Higher for complex apostille chains |
| Health insurance | €300–€800/year | Must cover Greece inpatient care |
| Translation and apostille | €100–€400 one-off | Depends on country of issuance |
| Travel to Greece for biometrics | Variable | One trip often covers whole family |

Full acquisition costs on the property itself, transfer tax at 3.09%, notary, and land registry, are covered in the [cost of buying property in Greece](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-greece/) guide. Parent permits add a few thousand euros to the immigration budget, not tens of thousands to the investment.

## Documents Parents Must Provide

Circular 1/2026 standardised the family file format. Parent applications reference the main investor's property file but require person-specific identity and relationship proof.

| Document | Purpose | Common pitfall |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Valid passport | Identity | Expiry within six months of appointment |
| Passport photographs | Biometric file | Wrong dimensions rejected |
| Investor birth certificate (apostilled) | Proves parent-child link | Name spelling mismatch across languages |
| Marriage certificate (for in-laws) | Proves spouse link | Missing apostille on older certificates |
| Family status certificate | Confirms marital status | Outdated certificate over six months old |
| Health insurance policy | Proves coverage in Greece | Travel policy without inpatient cover |
| Criminal record certificate | Background check | Wrong country of issuance |

Apostilles must come from the country that issued the original document. Greek authorities do not apostille foreign documents. Blended families, prior marriages, or name changes after naturalisation require lawyer review before submission. The [Golden Visa application timeline](/guides/greece-golden-visa-timeline-application-2026/) explains when to assemble these documents relative to deed registration.

## Health Insurance for Elderly Parents

Every family member, including parents in their seventies or eighties, must hold valid health insurance covering Greece at the time of application and renewal. Policies must include inpatient hospital coverage. Basic travel insurance without medical admission benefits is routinely rejected.

Practical options:

- International expatriate policies with explicit Greece territory coverage
- EU-compliant private policies purchased through brokers familiar with migration files
- Supplementary riders on existing home-country policies where insurers offer Schengen inpatient modules

Elderly applicants with pre-existing conditions face underwriting scrutiny. Start insurance quotes eight to twelve weeks before the biometric appointment so exclusions or loading do not delay the file. Your immigration lawyer should verify policy wording against Circular 1/2026 checklist language before you pay premiums.

## Biometrics, Mobility, and Practical Planning for Older Parents

Each parent must provide fingerprints and photographs at a Greek migration office unless a specific exception applies for severe mobility impairment, which is rare and case-by-case. Most families schedule one post-deed trip to Athens or Thessaloniki where the main applicant, spouse, children, and all parents attend sequential appointments on the same week.

Mobility planning matters for elderly parents:

- Airport assistance and ground-floor accommodation near the appointment location
- Wheelchair access at migration offices (Athens MoH offices vary; lawyer confirms venue)
- Medication schedules and doctor letters if travel is medically sensitive
- Power of attorney only for property steps, not a substitute for biometric attendance in most cases

During processing, authorities may issue a bridging confirmation allowing entry to Greece while the five-year card is printed. Schengen-wide travel before card issuance is restricted compared with the full permit. Do not book extended European travel for parents until your lawyer confirms which document they hold.

## Renewal and Property Dependency

Parent permits renew on the same five-year cycle as the investor. At renewal the investor must still hold a qualifying property meeting Law 5100/2024 rules: correct tier, single deed, at least 120 square metres for built residential tiers, no short-term rental use on the qualifying asset.

If the investor sells and replaces the property correctly before renewal, parent permits continue. If the qualifying investment lapses, all family permits, including parents, fail renewal. Details are in the [Golden Visa renewal requirements guide](/guides/greece-golden-visa-renewal-requirements/).

Parents do not re-prove financial dependency at renewal. The parental relationship documented at first application remains the qualifying factor unless marital status changes.

---

## Citizenship Reality Check for Parents

Golden Visa residency is not a citizenship shortcut. Naturalization requires seven consecutive years of legal physical residence in Greece, Greek language proficiency, civics examination, and integration evidence. Time spent abroad does not count even if the permit remains valid.

Families who want parents near them in Greece long term but do not plan seven years of physical presence should treat Golden Visa as residency and travel access, not a passport route. The [Golden Visa property guide 2026](/guides/greece-golden-visa-property-guide-2026/) covers programme scope; citizenship remains a separate legal pathway.

## Common Parent Application Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Assuming parents need separate property investment | Budget shock or abandoned application | Read family rules before property search |
| Using travel insurance instead of migration-grade policy | File rejection at biometrics | Verify inpatient Greece coverage wording |
| Name mismatch across passport and birth certificate | Weeks of consular reissue | Lawyer cross-check before apostille |
| Scheduling biometrics before deed registration | Appointment cancelled | Register property first |
| Omitting one set of in-laws | Family conflict and re-filing | Confirm both parent pairs early |

Families from countries with complex civil registry systems should start apostille work eight to ten weeks before target submission. The investor's birth certificate is the anchor document for both biological parents; the marriage certificate anchors in-law links to the spouse line.

---

## Schengen Travel After Parent Permits Issue

Once the five-year card is printed, parents gain Schengen mobility benefits comparable to other Golden Visa holders: extended stays in Greece and short trips across member states under long-stay residency norms. Rules for counting days in other Schengen countries vary; parents planning to split time between Greece and another EU country should confirm with counsel in both jurisdictions.

For healthcare beyond emergency cover, parents may use Greek private clinics or maintain home-country policies with EU riders. Budget annual check-ups and prescription continuity if parents manage chronic conditions abroad.

---

## Comparison With Other Family Routes in the EU

Greece is unusual in including four parents without extra capital. Portugal's post-reform Golden Visa no longer accepts residential property. Malta's permanent residence programme combines contribution and property with stricter presence rules. Cyprus programmes have shifted repeatedly and require careful legal review each year.

| Programme | Parents included? | Extra capital for parents? | Property route? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Greece Golden Visa | Yes (both sets) | No | Yes (direct deed) |
| Portugal Golden Visa | Limited family definition | N/A for property | No residential property |
| Malta PR | Depends on route | Often yes (contributions) | Lease or purchase hybrid |

If parent inclusion is your primary driver, Greece's structure is a material advantage, provided you accept the property retention requirement at every renewal. Compare programme mechanics in the [Golden Visa property guide 2026](/guides/greece-golden-visa-property-guide-2026/) before selecting a zone.
