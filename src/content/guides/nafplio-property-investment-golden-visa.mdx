Greek Golden Visa law requires one qualifying residential asset with minimum usable living area of 120 square metres on a single title deed. Nafplio's older building stock makes this rule more operationally important here than in purpose-built suburban developments.

**What counts.** Bedrooms, living rooms, kitchens, and bathrooms recorded as main usable space in the cadastral extract generally count toward the threshold.

**What does not count.** Open terraces, balconies counted separately, parking bays, basement storage, and common-area stairwells are excluded from the legal usable area figure buyers must prove to the Ministry of Migration.

**Single deed is mandatory.** Buying two adjacent old-town apartments on separate titles to reach 120m² combined fails the rule unless formally merged at the land registry before application submission. Merging heritage units can trigger engineering and permit costs that erase apparent savings.

**Off-plan and new build.** New apartments in outer Nafplio sometimes market at 95 to 110m² headline size. Verify the building permit and cadastral allocation before deposit. Developers occasionally include covered verandas in marketing m² that do not appear in the legal usable area field.
